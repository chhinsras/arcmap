County Economic Status in Appalachia Ohio (2020)

The map shows the county economic status of 32 counties in Appalachia Ohio. For context, there are 88 counties in Ohio, and 32 of them lie across the Appalachian region, notoriously known as the poor region in the U.S. To explain each economic status, the map will include attributes like the unemployment rate, median family income, and educational attainment rate, using the 5-year estimate data in 2019 from the U.S. Census Bureau.

  1. The purpose of the map

    The Appalachian Region Commission (ARC) has conducted a study to identify the economic status of all counties in the Appalachian region. The economic status include: (1) Distressed, (2) At-Risk, (3) Transitional, (4) Competitive, and (5) Attainment. However, the ARC’s map did not detail the reasons why each county is identified to particular economic status. Hence, my map will explore attributes that characterize each economic status using visual explanation and data from the U.S. Census Bureau. In the future, the map can also be used to explore the economic discrepancies between the Appalachia Ohio (32 counties) and the non-Appalachia Ohio (56 counties) in the state of Ohio. The map is for general audience

  2. The structure of the map:

    I want to create a thematic map that tells story about the county economic status in Appalachia Ohio (32 counties). For thematic techniques, I will use proportional symbols and Mercator projection. The geographic features in the map include (1) legend (2) title (3) sub-title (4) north arrow and (5) citation. In the legend, I will use rectangle boxes with different color density that correspond to each county on the map to describe the range of each data attribute I mentioned earlier. The map will have one main title (tab) that is the county economic status map from the ARC, and three sub-titles (tabs) that include (1) the median family income, (2) the educational attainment rate (both high school and the bachelor’s degree) and (3) the unemployment rate in those 32 counties in Appalachian Ohio. Hence, you will see four tabs in my map, and as you click on each tab, you will see the visual display of each attribute that correspond with the map.

  3. Data sources:

    I have collected the data of the attributes, as was discussed earlier, in the 32 counties of the Appalachia Ohio. They include (1) the median family income, (2) the educational attainment rate (both high school and bachelor’s degree), and (3) the unemployment rate, using the ACS (American Community Survey) 5-year estimates data. The reason I chose the 5-year estimates data is because of its larger sample size and greater accuracy, as compared to the 1-year estimates data.

  4. Methodology:

    I have created the tabular CSV data of all the attributes mentioned. I will insert that CSV data into the map that I generate on online ArcGis.
